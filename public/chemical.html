Overview

Chemical engineering is the application of chemistry and engineering principles to production processes. Engineers design and optimize processes for manufacturing chemicals, fuels, and materials at scale.

Roadmap

  • Foundations: Chemistry, calculus, physics, materials science
  • Core: Thermodynamics, transport phenomena, reaction engineering
  • Advanced: Process design, safety, capstone projects

Subfields

Process Engineering

Design and optimization of chemical production processes including reactors, separation systems, and heat exchangers. Process engineers develop efficient, safe, and economical methods for manufacturing chemicals at scale.

Top Companies:

Dow Chemical, BASF, ExxonMobil Chemical, DuPont

Petroleum & Energy

Development of technologies for oil and gas extraction, refining, and energy production. Engineers work on improving extraction efficiency, reducing environmental impact, and developing alternative energy sources.

Top Companies:

Shell, Chevron, BP, ConocoPhillips, Saudi Aramco

Environmental Engineering

Treatment of air, water, and waste streams to minimize environmental impact. Environmental chemical engineers develop pollution control technologies and sustainable manufacturing processes.

Top Companies:

Veolia, Suez, Waste Management, Covanta Energy

Materials Processing

Processing and fabrication of advanced materials including polymers, composites, and ceramics. Chemical engineers optimize material properties through processing techniques and chemical modifications.

Top Companies:

3M, Arkema, Huntsman, LyondellBasell

Biochemical Engineering

Application of chemical engineering principles to biological systems for producing biofuels, biochemicals, and biologics. These engineers scale up bioprocesses for industrial production.

Top Companies:

Genentech, Amgen, Moderna, Biogen

Pharmaceutical Engineering

Design and optimization of manufacturing processes for drug production and delivery systems. Pharmaceutical engineers ensure drug quality, safety, and consistency at industrial scales.

Top Companies:

Pfizer, Merck, Johnson & Johnson, Roche, AbbVie
